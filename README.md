# 📊 DCF Valuation Models — Repository Overview

A structured collection of Excel-based Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation models covering multiple public companies across industries. Each spreadsheet combines business research, financial normalization, forecasting, and valuation outputs into a single analytical workflow.

The repository is designed as both:

- a repeatable valuation framework, and
- a research archive documenting assumptions behind each model.

---

## 🧭 Repository Structure

### 📂 Individual Company Models (Excel)

Each spreadsheet represents a complete valuation case for a single company. While companies differ, all files follow a consistent architecture:

**1) Main**

- Company snapshot: sector, operations, listing, scale, market position
- Capital structure overview: price, shares, market cap, debt, cash, enterprise value
- Key context notes and high-level investment framing

**2) Model**

- Historical financial normalization
- Revenue and operating projections
- Free Cash Flow (FCF) construction
- Terminal value assumptions
- Discounting logic and NPV outputs
- Equity value and implied valuation ranges

**3) WACC**

- Cost of equity estimation
- Cost of debt assumptions
- Capital structure weighting
- Final discount rate used in valuation

**4) Additional analytical sheets (select models)**
Depending on company and research depth:

- Macroeconomic context (inflation, interest rates)
- Statistical relationships and regressions
- Market benchmarks
- Sensitivity views and charts
- Definitions and accounting clarifications

---

## 🔬 What Makes These Models Different

These spreadsheets are not template-only DCFs. They incorporate:

- Company-specific accounting adjustments
- Business model interpretation before forecasting
- Explicit links between macro variables and projections (where relevant)
- Capital structure–aware valuation rather than simplified shortcuts
- Research notes embedded directly into the modeling environment

The intent is analytical transparency: every valuation output is traceable to an operational assumption.

---

## 🧠 Core Methodology

All models are built around fundamental, cash-driven valuation:

**Free Cash Flow Construction**

- Derived from operating performance rather than accounting earnings
- Incorporates working capital behavior and reinvestment intensity

**Forecasting Logic**

- Revenue drivers tied to business realities (not mechanical growth rates)
- Margins modeled through operational leverage and industry structure

**Discounting Framework**

- WACC-based approach reflecting risk, leverage, and market conditions
- Company-specific adjustments where capital structure or volatility requires it

**Terminal Value**

- Perpetuity growth method used as baseline
- Growth rates constrained by macroeconomic realism and industry maturity

**Valuation Output**

- Enterprise value → equity value bridge
- Comparison with market capitalization
- Implied upside/downside framed as a function of assumptions

## 📈 Research Philosophy

This repository treats DCF not as a formula, but as a thinking process.

Each model attempts to answer:

1. How the business actually makes money
2. What must happen operationally for growth to occur
3. What capital is required to sustain that growth
4. How risk should be priced
5. Whether the current market valuation reflects those realities

The spreadsheets therefore function as:

- research notebooks,
- decision frameworks,
- and long-term valuation records.

---

## 🎯 Purpose

**Primary goal:** build a consistent, fundamentals-first valuation system applicable across industries.

**Secondary goals:**

- improve modeling discipline through repetition and comparison
- document reasoning behind each assumption
- create a reusable structure for future company analysis
- maintain a transparent track record of valuation logic

This is not a short-term trading tool.
It is a long-horizon intrinsic value framework grounded in cash generation, capital allocation, and business fundamentals.

---

## 🧪 How to Use the Models

1. Start with **Main** for company context and capital structure.
2. Move to **Model** to understand forecast logic and cash flow construction.
3. Review **WACC** to evaluate discount assumptions.
4. Explore supporting sheets for macro inputs, statistical context, and sensitivity.
5. Compare implied intrinsic value with market pricing.

Each file is self-contained and can be used independently, while still following the same valuation architecture as the rest of the repository.
